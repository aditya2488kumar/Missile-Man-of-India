A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he studied physics and aerospace engineering and became a key figure in India's space and missile programs, earning the title "Missile Man of India." He played a crucial role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998. Elected president with bipartisan support, he was known as the "People's President." After his presidency, he focused on education and writing. Kalam passed away in 2015 while delivering a lecture, and was honored with a state funeral in his hometown.

Contribution for india

  1. Led the development of SLV-III, India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle, launching the Rohini satellite in 1980
  2. Directed the evolution of PSLV (Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle) program at ISRO
  3. versaw the development of ballistic missiles through projects Devil and Valiant
  4. Led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP), resulting in the Agni and Prithvi missiles
  5. Played a key role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, enhancing India's nuclear security
